Beautiful Gardens

Deep dive into the different areas at Butchart Gardens

Sunken Garden

Butchart Gardens is located on the vancouver island, British Columbia. This garden is 121 years old, created by Jennie Butchart with help from her husband. She created a beautiful garden from a previous limestone quarry. Which 121 years later has bloomed into the beautiful landscape seen in the image above and popular tourist site.

Japanese Garden

Butchart Gardens has many different areas inside its main garden. One of which is the Japanese garden. Going through this section of the garden, you are welcomed with a classic Japanese Tori Gate. Within the gate there is forests of bamboo, zen gardens made of sand, and small Japanese bridges.

Italian Garden

Entering a small tunnel surounded by bushes, there is a little court, surrounded by buildings. The focal point of this section in the gardens is the massive foutain covered in lilypads lines with bunches of pink and purple flowers. All the buildings are covered in growing plants. The whole section is very clean and kept more particular then the rest of the gardens.
